# Mental Wellness Conversational Trees

This file defines the branching conversation paths for the mental wellness chatbot.

---

## TREE 1: CONSTANT OVERTHINKING
**Root Question:** I feel like I’m constantly overthinking everything. How do I stop this?
**Response:** Overthinking is often a loop where the mind tries to solve an unsolvable or uncertain problem. It’s like the "check-engine" light of your brain being stuck on.
**Follow-up 1:** What kind of thoughts repeat the most in your mind? (Work, Relationships, Future, Past)
**Follow-up 2:** Does this overthinking happen mostly when you are alone or around people?
**Follow-up 3:** Do you feel like you need to have a "perfect" answer for everything?

### BRANCH: Future Worries
**Trigger:** Future
**Response:** Worrying about the future is your brain's attempt to protect you from uncertainty. However, we can't control everything.
**Follow-up:** Do you find yourself imagining the worst-case scenario often?

### BRANCH: Past Mistakes
**Trigger:** Past
**Response:** Dwelling on the past is your brain trying to "fix" something that has already happened. It’s important to practice self-forgiveness.
**Follow-up:** Do you feel like you are being too hard on yourself for things you can't change?

---

## TREE 2: MIND RACING AT NIGHT
**Root Question:** My mind doesn’t stop thinking even at night. What can I do?
**Response:** Nighttime is when external distractions disappear, and your brain finally has the quiet to process everything it couldn't during the day.
**Follow-up 1:** Do you use your phone or laptop right before you try to sleep?
**Follow-up 2:** Do your thoughts feel like "to-do lists" or "what-if" worries?
**Follow-up 3:** Have you tried any relaxation or breathing techniques yet?

### BRANCH: Digital Stimulation
**Trigger:** Phone/Laptop
**Response:** The blue light and constant information intake from screens tell your brain it’s still daytime, keeping it in an active state.
**Follow-up:** Would you be open to trying a "digital sunset" 30 minutes before bed?

---

## TREE 3: MENTAL EXHAUSTION
**Root Question:** I feel mentally exhausted all the time. Why does this happen?
**Response:** Mental exhaustion occurs when your "cognitive battery" is drained by too many decisions, constant stress, or emotional labor without enough recharge time.
**Follow-up 1:** Have you been taking significant breaks from work or social media lately?
**Follow-up 2:** Is your exhaustion paired with feeling unmotivated or bored?
**Follow-up 3:** Are you getting at least 7 hours of sleep on most nights?

### BRANCH: High Stress
**Trigger:** Work/Stress
**Response:** Constant high stress keeps your brain in "fight or flight" mode, which is incredibly draining over long periods.
**Follow-up:** Do you feel like you have a way to "turn off" your work brain at home?

---

## TREE 4: MAGNIFIED PROBLEMS
**Root Question:** Why do small problems feel like huge ones in my mind?
**Response:** This is called "catastrophizing." When your background stress levels are already high, your brain loses its ability to prioritize, and every tiny spark feels like a forest fire.
**Follow-up 1:** Do you feel like you are already "full" of other stressors right now?
**Follow-up 2:** Does this feeling come on suddenly or build up throughout the day?
**Follow-up 3:** Do you notice physical symptoms like a racing heart or shallow breathing?

---

## TREE 5: CALMING THE OVERWHELMED MIND
**Root Question:** How can I calm my mind when I feel overwhelmed?
**Response:** The first step is to bring your brain back from the "thinking" world into the "sensing" world. Grounding yourself in the present moment is key.
**Follow-up 1:** Where are you right now? Can you name 3 things you see?
**Follow-up 2:** Are you noticing any tension in your shoulders or jaw right now?
**Follow-up 3:** Would you like me to guide you through a quick 4-7-8 breathing exercise?

### BRANCH: Physical Tension
**Trigger:** Tension
**Response:** Physical tension is the body's way of storing mental stress. Deep breathing can signal the nervous system to relax.
**Follow-up:** Try dropping your shoulders and unclencing your jaw. Does that help a little?
